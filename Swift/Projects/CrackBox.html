CrackBox

I created 'PostUp' a few months before my 10th grade exams as an accessible website for myself that stored all my e-notes. Eventually, a lot of my classmates were asking for my notes and posters, and that's when I realised that the internet actually lacks good quality learning resources. Most of the learning material out there is actually black and white; and almost all of the good quality stuff is either paid or extremely difficult to find. And so, I came up with the idea to launch PostUp as resource bank, not just for me, but now for everyone.

At the same time, I reached my 11th grade and I was introduced to CAS. And so I took the opportunity and began working on PostUp as my CAS Project. I had to eventually rename the project to CrackBox because PostUp's domain was unavailable. I then brought together a team of 11th grade students and all of us began working on CrackBox. We piloted CrackBox towards becoming a self sustaining community that allows learners to share their learning material on a global platform. We also developed our own material and moderate everything on our website to make CrackBox the one stop solution for the best quality learning aid out there!

We started off with our iconic poster library, featuring several beautiful and educational posters that capture entire topics into a crisp and condensed earning lamp posts. A term I like to use to describe CrackBox posters because of how powerful of a revision tool they can be!

We have evolved a lot from just a simple poster library, we have introduced other sub-divisions like 'Textbooks Remastered', 'flashcards', etc. and we also have projects like 'CrackBox Plus' in the making. But if you wanna know more about CrackBox, just hit the button below and visit the website!
