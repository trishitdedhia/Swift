DBIS Game Design Challenge 2020
powered by CrackBox

In 2020, during the Covid-19 pandemic, the lockdown had caused so many children to start playing video games for extremely long durations everyday! It had become another pandemic for the parents altogether! And so I though, instead of letting them play video games all day, why don't we encourage them to make video games? So, as a launching event for CrackBox, I organised the first ever, Inter House Game Design Competition in my school!

(If you don't know what CrackBox is, click here)

Over the course of a month, the participants had to create an educational game. The winning games would be displayed on the CrackBox website's front page; plus, the winners would be earning points for their own houses. Students from grades 6 to 11 could participate individually or in teams upto 4 people max. And for the judging, the teams then got to present their games on an open webinar in front of a panel of esteemed judges.

The response for the competition was fantastic in terms of a stepping stone. Encouraging students to get into something like game development requires a different atmosphere and ambience to be set. At the first go, you can't expect them to come up with fully functional and stunning games. But year after year, their work gets better and eventually the DGDC becomes a part of the schools culture. This is when the competitiveness allows students to do better every time. In the years to come, I hope the DGDC can do just that, creating a healthy atmosphere of competition, productivity and learning, encouraging all students to pursue game design, at least, as a hobby.
